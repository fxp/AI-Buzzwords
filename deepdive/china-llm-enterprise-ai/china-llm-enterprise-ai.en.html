TL;DR / 30-SECOND CORE

Anthropic's framework provides a deconstructible sample, but the Chinese version requires a full rewrite across three dimensions—capital structure, compliance signals, and digital sovereignty—and once rewritten, it may actually form a deeper moat than the US version.

01

Anthropic's three-layer structure (JV channel + pre-built agents + compliance endorsement) is transferable, but all three elements require local substitutes in China

02

China's "Dimon equivalent" for compliance signals is not an entrepreneur—it's the president of ICBC/CCB or an actual deployment case from a regulatory body; harder to obtain, but once secured, the moat is deeper

Counter-Consensus Insight

The real competitors for Chinese LLM enterprises are not each other, nor OpenAI/Claude—they are software companies like Wind, JZ Technology, and Yonyou Network that have already built industry data pipelines. Whoever captures them first seizes the entry point to China's Bloomberg Terminal equivalent.

Underestimated Fact

In scenarios like government/regulatory reporting, foreign competitors are effectively absent—this matters more than any parameter advantage.

Single Most Important Recommendation

Pick a vertical with "highly standardized formats + data localization requirements + heavy regulatory pressure," and complete the industry JV + pre-built agents + industry endorsement three-layer connection—the first to do so becomes the Anthropic of the Chinese market.

§ 01 / FRAMEWORK

Deconstructing
Anthropic's Three-Layer
Structure

What Anthropic accomplished in 72 hours was not three independent things—it was a tightly coupled three-layer system. Understanding this system is the prerequisite for transplanting it to the Chinese market.

LAYER 1 / Distribution Pipeline
PE JV ($1.5B)—Blackstone + H&F + Goldman Sachs and 5 other institutions
Function: access to enterprise clients + systems integration entry point · Use of funds: roll-up existing AI services firms (per Reuters), not "doing consulting"
↓
LAYER 2 / Product Shelf
10 Pre-Built Financial Agents—PitchBook analysis / KYC / monthly close / compliance audit / M&A summaries…
Deployable directly into enterprise workflows; not demos, no custom development needed
↓
LAYER 3 / Compliance Credit
Jamie Dimon's public endorsement—JPMorgan is a Project Glasswing co-founding member
Real meaning: JPMorgan's legal team has reviewed it—using it won't get a compliance officer fired

Key Insight: Strong dependencies exist between the three layers—without Layer 3 (compliance credit), enterprises won't buy; without Layer 2 (concrete products), the JV has nothing to sell; without Layer 1 (distribution pipeline), products can't reach decision-makers. The problem for Chinese LLM enterprises is often that all three layers are missing simultaneously, or each exists in isolation without connecting into a system.

§ 02 / DIFFERENCES

US–China Enterprise AI
Difference Map

Difference 01 / Procurement Decision Chain
🇺🇸 United States

CTO / CFO joint decision, 2–6 month cycle, technical evaluation carries high weight

🇨🇳 China

Procurement committee + IT department + compliance department + (for SOEs) Party committee, 6–18 month cycle, qualifications, classified protection compliance, and domestic certification carry equally high weight

→ No matter how good the product, missing Level 3 classified protection + domestic certification + SOE precedent may cost you the deal

Difference 02 / Source of Compliance Signals
🇺🇸 United States

Dimon's endorsement = JPMorgan's legal team reviewed it = industry admission ticket. A private-sector CEO's endorsement suffices

🇨🇳 China

Requires a use case from an institution with regulatory lineage. ICBC/CCB president endorsing = China's Dimon equivalent; harder to obtain, but once secured the lock-in effect is stronger (SOEs typically don't procure competing products simultaneously)

→ Compliance signals are harder to obtain, but the resulting moat depth far exceeds the US version

Difference 03 / Digital Sovereignty Constraints ← Most Important
🇺🇸 United States

Financial data can go on AWS/Azure private clouds; cross-border data flows are relatively free. Anthropic faces global competition from OpenAI, Gemini, etc.

🇨🇳 China

Data Security Law + Measures for Security Assessment of Cross-Border Data Transfers: core financial/medical/government data effectively cannot leave the country. Foreign LLMs cannot process this data

→ Foreign models absent = de facto monopoly for domestic LLMs in core financial/government/medical scenarios. This is the competitive landscape Anthropic envies most: geographic borders are the moat.

Difference 04 / Distribution Channel Structure

US: Independent PE networks (Blackstone holds 2,700+ enterprises) + Accenture/Deloitte systems integration

China: No equivalent independent PE enterprise network. SI backbone is Huawei / Yonyou / Kingdee / Glodon; pipelines are Alibaba Cloud/Tencent Cloud/Huawei Cloud/Tianyi Cloud

→ China's JV counterpart = Huawei Cloud + an SOE fund / Yonyou + ICBC Strategic Investment Dept

Difference 05 / Competitive Landscape

US: Anthropic vs OpenAI vs Gemini, globalized competition, sensitive verticals also open

China: Core verticals (government/financial regulation/medical) have foreign players effectively absent; only domestic vendors compete

→ Competition is more concentrated, the pie more consolidated, and homogenization risk is higher

Difference 06 / SaaS Penetration & Large-Enterprise-Dominated Landscape
🇺🇸 United States

SaaS penetration ~40%, mixed SME + large enterprise market, cloud-native as default deployment. Anthropic's agents deliver via API / cloud subscription with no on-prem needed; enterprises can self-serve

🇨🇳 China

SaaS penetration ~10%–15%, procurement budgets heavily concentrated in central SOEs, state-owned enterprises, and top private companies. Large enterprises universally require on-premises deployment (localization)—data must not leave their own data centers; this is a hard requirement, not a negotiating chip

→ The China-version pre-built agent SKU must include three deployment forms: cloud / on-prem / domestic xinchuang edition; pure cloud SaaS cannot enter core scenarios

Difference 07 / Yonyou · Kingdee · Glodon: The Underestimated Hidden Barrier

The US distribution barrier is SIs (Accenture/Deloitte). Chinese large enterprises' workflows are deeply embedded in ERP / industry management software, creating a harder barrier to bypass:

Yonyou YonBIP

Covers 7 million enterprises, top choice for large manufacturing / SOE ERP, already has built-in YonGPT—Yonyou is growing AI capabilities from inside ERP; it's a competitor, not just a distribution channel

Kingdee Cloud · Cosmic

Ultra-large enterprise ERP platform, deep partnership with Huawei Cloud, already has AI agent capability framework. Existing data + workflow integration + customer trust—triple barrier stacked

Glodon

Monopoly platform for construction industry digitization, now AI-ifying engineering budgets / BIM scenarios. External LLMs can barely bypass it to access engineering digitization data

Core Judgment: Large enterprises' AI procurement decisions will likely ultimately come down to "whether to turn on AI inside Yonyou/Kingdee"—not "whether to purchase an AI product." LLM enterprises need a clear answer: embed (become the AI layer inside ERP) or replace (build AI-native ERP)? The business models, sales motions, and capital structures of the two paths are fundamentally different.
